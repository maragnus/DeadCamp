## Core pitch

**A co-op Roblox survival road-trip game where players escape a zombie outbreak in an RV, scavenging campsites, gas stations, ranger stations, farms, and abandoned towns while defending the vehicle from hordes at night.**

Instead of train-western vibes, lean into:

* **Modern camping horror**
* **Road-trip survival**
* **RV maintenance**
* **Friend-group chaos**
* **“We are absolutely not qualified for this” energy**

## Better framing

> A zombie outbreak spreads across the wilderness. Players pile into a battered RV and travel toward a rumored safe haven beyond the mountains. Along the way, they must loot supplies, repair the RV, manage fuel, survive nights, and fight off increasingly dangerous infected.

There. Same broad appeal, less “Dead Rails with cupholders.”

## Main gameplay loop

### 1. Drive

Players travel along a semi-random road route in the RV.

Possible route stops:

* Campgrounds
* Gas stations
* Abandoned cabins
* National park checkpoints
* Small towns
* Roadblocks
* Ranger towers
* Tunnels
* Military quarantine zones

### 2. Stop and loot

Players exit the RV to grab:

* Fuel
* Food
* Ammo
* Medicine
* Scrap
* Batteries
* Weapons
* RV parts
* Quest items

Looting should be risky. Zombies nearby, limited time, maybe alarms. Because apparently humanity needed *more* reasons to make gas stations unpleasant.

### 3. Repair and upgrade the RV

The RV should be the “train” equivalent, but more personal and funny.

Systems:

* **Fuel tank**
* **Engine health**
* **Tire durability**
* **Battery power**
* **Barricades**
* **Storage**
* **Lights**
* **Mounted weapons**
* **Interior crafting bench**
* **Medical station**

Upgrades:

| Upgrade            | Effect                             |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------- |
| Reinforced windows | Zombies take longer to break in    |
| Roof turret        | One player can defend while moving |
| Larger fuel tank   | Longer travel distance             |
| Solar panels       | Recharge battery during day        |
| Armor plating      | Reduces crash/zombie damage        |
| Storage racks      | Carry more loot                    |
| Bunk beds          | Faster healing while parked        |
| Floodlights        | Reveal zombies at night            |

## Roles for players

Give players informal jobs so multiplayer feels useful instead of four gremlins fighting over one shotgun.

| Role      | Job                              |
| --------- | -------------------------------- |
| Driver    | Controls RV and route choices    |
| Scout     | Searches ahead and marks loot    |
| Mechanic  | Repairs and upgrades RV          |
| Medic     | Heals, revives, manages supplies |
| Defender  | Handles weapons and barricades   |
| Navigator | Chooses stops and safe routes    |

Don’t force classes too hard. Roblox players enjoy freedom, chaos, and making every system worse in creative ways.

## Zombie types

Keep them readable and mechanically distinct.

| Zombie   | Behavior                       |
| -------- | ------------------------------ |
| Walker   | Basic slow enemy               |
| Sprinter | Fast, low health               |
| Crawler  | Hard to see, attacks ankles    |
| Bloater  | Explodes near RV               |
| Screamer | Attracts hordes                |
| Climber  | Gets onto RV roof              |
| Brute    | Smashes barricades             |
| Stalker  | Appears at night, avoids light |

## Night defense phase

This should be a major tension point.

At night, the RV may need to stop because:

* Road is blocked
* Engine overheats
* Driver needs rest
* Bridge is out
* Storm hits
* Military checkpoint shuts down

Players must defend while:

* Repairing barricades
* Managing ammo
* Keeping lights powered
* Fighting zombies
* Fixing the RV before sunrise

## Random events

These will make runs feel different.

* Bridge collapse
* Forest fire
* Zombie deer, because nature wanted in on the stupidity
* Loot convoy crash
* Bandit ambush
* Lost survivor asking for help
* Fake safe zone
* RV stuck in mud
* Thunderstorm disables lights
* Tunnel full of infected
* Gas station alarm attracts horde
* Campsite with “free supplies” trap

## Safe haven goal

The safe haven should be distant and slightly mysterious.

Possible destinations:

* Mountain bunker
* Island ferry terminal
* Military evacuation zone
* Walled survivor town
* Radio tower broadcasting coordinates
* Research camp with a cure
* Underground shelter

The journey matters more than the ending. But give players a clear objective:

> Travel 100 miles to reach Haven Ridge before the infection overwhelms the region.

## Progression

For Roblox retention, you probably want both **run-based survival** and **persistent unlocks**.

### During a run

Players collect:

* Supplies
* Weapons
* Scrap
* Temporary upgrades
* Gas
* Medicine

### Between runs

Players unlock:

* New RV skins
* Starting perks
* Cosmetic backpacks
* New routes
* Better starter tools
* Emotes
* Titles
* Camp decorations
* Pets, if you must feed the algorithm beast

Avoid pay-to-win. Sell cosmetics, effects, emotes, RV skins, horn sounds, flashlight skins, victory poses. Let the children honk a clown horn during the apocalypse. Civilization is already gone.

## Make it feel different from Dead Rails

Lean into things Dead Rails does not own:

* RV interior gameplay
* Camping survival
* Route voting
* Vehicle breakdowns
* Campfire/rest phases
* Weather
* Wilderness horror
* Buddy roles
* Temporary camps
* Moral choices with survivors
* Road-trip radio broadcasts
* RV customization

## Simple game structure

```text
Lobby
  ↓
Choose RV + difficulty + route
  ↓
Start road trip
  ↓
Drive segment
  ↓
Random stop
  ↓
Loot / fight / repair
  ↓
Night defense
  ↓
Upgrade RV
  ↓
Continue toward safe haven
  ↓
Final escape event
```

## Strong hook

The RV should feel like the team’s shared home.

Players should yell things like:

* “Who forgot fuel?”
* “Why is there a zombie on the roof?”
* “Stop driving, I’m still outside!”
* “Who used all the medkits?”
* “Why did you sell the engine part?”

That is the actual multiplayer magic. Not zombies. Not guns. Just friends making terrible decisions together, as humanity has practiced for centuries.
